# Preface

It all started when I went to attend TinkerDay Kozhikode '25, where we had the chance to listen to one of the talks of Varsha Shaheen. She told us about her B. Tech journey - Starting from 1st year, publishing a simple research paper and travelling throughout India to attend and present at different conferences.

The idea of researching and travelling peaked my interest at that time, but I kept it hidden. One day, I randomnly asked one of classmates who had also attended the session - whether she would be ready to collaborate and research together and to my surprise - she was also thinking of the same.

On that day itself, we went to meet on my favourite faculty - Sajith Miss to ask for guidance. She was so helpful and was actually giving motivating yet realistic way forward. She asked us to finalise our research topic and read a lot of research papers. We started doing so... 5 months went by, and we had little to no progress, but I learned some things the I wish I had known earlier and thought it might help anyone like me. So here we go!

# The tools

1. [Google scholar](https://scholar.google.com/) - The search engine for research paper:
   Honestly, you should start here. Try searching for your research keywords here. You should be able to see and access the research papers written by others and it will help you set your research topic. Read those papers and find the gap that you contribute to.
2. [ONOS](https://www.onos.gov.in/) - This is a scheme by central government scheme to promote researching among students:
   It gives access to a lot of websites which publishes research papers like ScienceDirect. Free access for all. See access guide: https://www.onos.gov.in/how-to-access
3. [Research](https://un.ms/research) by unms - A desktop app to keep you organised

I will be updating this blog, including my personal experiences and also the tools I use along the way. If you want to contribute to this, please do reach out to me on LinkedIn :)
